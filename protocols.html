How to use this document

This is a working draft of evidence-based defaults for the adult inpatient phase (POD 0 through discharge) of liver, kidney, and pancreas transplantation. It is organized so each section can be lifted directly into an Epic SmartPhrase. The companion .txt file contains the same content already formatted with @SmartList@ and *** wildcards.

Conventions used throughout

  • [CENTER-SPECIFIC] — a decision point where institutional protocol typically governs (e.g., choice of CNI target, induction intensity).
  • [STRENGTH] — strength of recommendation per the cited guideline (e.g., KDIGO 1A, AST strong).
  • *** — Epic free-text wildcard.
  • @SmartList@ — Epic pick-list placeholder.

Important caveats before using

  • This document is a starting framework, not a substitute for institutional protocol or surgeon order. Doses, durations, and CNI targets vary materially between centers.
  • Where two acceptable strategies exist (e.g., basiliximab vs. rATG induction), the doc lists both and flags the choice rather than asserting a single answer.
  • Cite-checking is the responsibility of the implementing clinician; guidelines update on a schedule independent of this draft.

Cross-cutting principles (all three organs)

1. Immunosuppression — shared backbone

All three organs use a calcineurin-inhibitor based maintenance regimen. The standard is tacrolimus + an antimetabolite (mycophenolate mofetil or mycophenolic acid) ± corticosteroids. This is consistent across KDIGO 2009 (kidney), AASLD 2013 (liver), and AST/ASTS pancreas guidance.

  • Tacrolimus — start when GI tract functional and renal function permits. Typical starting dose 0.05–0.1 mg/kg PO BID, titrated to trough. Targets differ by organ (see organ sections).
  • Mycophenolate mofetil (MMF) — 1000 mg PO BID standard adult dose; reduce for cytopenias or GI intolerance (500–750 mg BID). MPA delayed-release equivalent: 720 mg BID.
  • Corticosteroids — methylprednisolone intraop (typically 500–1000 mg), then taper over POD 1–5 to oral prednisone. Long-term steroid use vs. early withdrawal is [CENTER-SPECIFIC].

Evidence: KDIGO 2009 Care of the Kidney Transplant Recipient (Chapter 1 — Induction; Chapter 2 — Initial maintenance) is the most rigorous available framework and is broadly extrapolated to liver and pancreas in the absence of equivalent organ-specific RCT-driven guidelines.

2. Drug–drug interactions to flag at admission

  • CYP3A4 inhibitors (azole antifungals, macrolides, diltiazem, verapamil, grapefruit) — dramatically increase tacrolimus levels. Anticipate dose reduction of 50–75% when starting fluconazole/voriconazole.
  • CYP3A4 inducers (rifampin, phenytoin, carbamazepine, St. John's wort) — reduce tacrolimus exposure; usually avoid.
  • QT prolongers stack with tacrolimus, voriconazole, ondansetron, fluoroquinolones — baseline ECG and electrolyte management.

3. VTE / GI / stress ulcer prophylaxis

Post-transplant VTE risk is meaningfully elevated above general surgical baseline. Mechanical prophylaxis (sequential compression devices) starts at admission. Pharmacologic prophylaxis timing is the trade-off point — earlier = less VTE, later = less surgical-site bleeding.

  • Mechanical: SCDs on admission, continued until ambulating well.
  • Pharmacologic: unfractionated heparin 5000 units SC q12h [LOCAL PROTOCOL: attending preference at this center is q12h, not the textbook q8h]; LMWH (enoxaparin 40 mg SC daily) acceptable if renal function adequate. Start time differs by organ — see organ-specific sections.
  • Stress ulcer / steroid GI prophylaxis: PPI (e.g., pantoprazole 40 mg IV/PO daily) while on high-dose steroids; reassess at steroid taper completion.
  • Bowel regimen: docusate ± senna scheduled; aggressive opioid-induced constipation prevention (constipation drives ileus, which delays IS absorption).

4. Infection prophylaxis — baseline framework

Three layers of antimicrobial prophylaxis apply across all three organs, with organ-specific modifications layered on top: (a) CMV based on D/R serostatus, (b) PJP, (c) candida/oral. For CMV, the Fourth International Consensus on the Management of CMV in SOT (Kotton et al., Transplantation 2025) is now the authoritative reference — it endorses letermovir as an alternative first-line option for D+/R− kidney recipients (FDA approved June 2023) alongside valganciclovir. For PJP and Candida, the AST IDCOP 2019 guidelines remain current. BK virus screening for kidney recipients is governed by the Second International BK Consensus (Kotton et al., Transplantation 2024) — covered in the Kidney section.

5. Local center conventions [LOCAL PROTOCOL]

These are attending preferences at this center that diverge from textbook defaults. They are not derived from KDIGO/AASLD/AST guidelines; they reflect local consensus and should be honored unless the responsible attending says otherwise on a given patient.

  • SQ heparin frequency: q12h, not q8h. When pharmacologic VTE prophylaxis is appropriate, order unfractionated heparin 5000 units SC every 12 hours. This applies to all three organs.
  • ASA on the post-op order set: HOLD by default. Uncheck the ASA box on the post-op transplant order set. The attending decides at the post-op debrief when ASA is appropriate to start. (A hard-check removal request is in with the Epic order-set team; uncheck in the meantime.) For pancreas recipients, the eventual transition off heparin gtt onto ASA 81 mg is also an attending decision, not an automatic switch.
  • IV methylprednisolone taper continues even when thymo is held. Universal taper: 500 mg in OR → 250 mg POD #1 → 125 mg POD #2 → then PO prednisone taper. If thymo (rATG) is held for thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, or hypotension, continue the IV taper through the 125 mg dose, then transition to 62.5 mg IV daily as a bridge until the thymo course is complete. If the patient gets only 1 dose of thymo post-op, continue the IV MP taper as scheduled rather than abbreviating it.
  • MMF discharge dose is individualized — not a blanket 500 mg BID. Default discharge MMF at this center is often 500 mg BID, but bump to 1 g PO BID for any of: highly sensitized recipient, DSA-positive recipient, high-BMI recipient, tacrolimus not yet therapeutic at discharge. Ask the attending at discharge if any uncertainty.

How this is rendered downstream: Throughout the organ-specific sections that follow, items conforming to these four conventions are tagged [LOCAL PROTOCOL]. This makes it easy to see which choices are guideline-derived vs. attending preference, and to swap them out if the document is shared with a different program.

Liver transplant — adult inpatient

Adult deceased-donor whole liver transplant is the dominant case mix; living-donor and split-liver recipients have meaningfully different bleeding/biliary risk profiles and warrant attending-level adjustments.

Liver — immunosuppression

Induction

  • Most adult liver transplant programs do NOT use induction routinely. Induction is reserved for renal-sparing protocols (delayed CNI start) and immunologic high-risk recipients. [CENTER-SPECIFIC]
  • Renal-sparing / delayed-CNI protocol: basiliximab 20 mg IV on POD 0 and POD 4, with tacrolimus introduction delayed until creatinine improves (e.g., Cr <2 or off RRT).
  • Lymphocyte-depleting induction (rATG) is uncommon in standard liver transplantation — typically restricted to retransplant or autoimmune indications.

Maintenance

  • Tacrolimus starting 0.05–0.1 mg/kg PO BID once GI function present (POD 1–2); IV form (continuous infusion) avoided when possible due to neurotoxicity.
  • Tacrolimus trough targets (typical): 8–10 ng/mL during month 1; 6–8 ng/mL months 2–3; 5–8 ng/mL after month 3. [CENTER-SPECIFIC]
  • MMF 1000 mg PO BID; continued unless cytopenias/GI.
  • Steroid taper [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: methylprednisolone 500 mg intraop → 250 mg IV POD 1 → 125 mg IV POD 2 → then PO prednisone taper to 5–10 mg. Withdrawal of steroids is supported but timing matters: the ILTS-ILCA 2024 consensus and recent RCT data favor withdrawal at month 3 rather than at 2 weeks (NODAT incidence ~10% vs ~32% at 2-week withdrawal). Maintain steroids longer in autoimmune indications (PBC/PSC/AIH) and high-risk recipients.
  • If thymoglobulin (rATG) is being used and is held mid-course (for thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, or hypotension), continue the IV MP taper through 125 mg, then transition to 62.5 mg IV daily until the thymo course is complete. If the patient receives only 1 dose of thymo post-op, continue the IV MP taper as scheduled. [LOCAL PROTOCOL — uncommon in liver transplant since induction is rare, but applies if used]

Evidence: AASLD/AST Practice Guideline. Adult Liver Transplantation: Diagnosis and Management of Graft-Related Complications. Te HS et al. Liver Transpl. 2025;32(3):444–490 (now formally published; supersedes the interim Hepatology 2024 online version and Lucey 2013). ILTS-ILCA Consensus Recommendations for Liver Transplantation and Transplant Oncology. Liver Transplantation. 2025;31(6). EASL Clinical Practice Guidelines on Liver Transplantation. J Hepatol. 2024 (October). The Lucey 2013 AASLD/AST guideline and Charlton 2018 ILTS consensus remain useful historical references but are superseded for current practice.

Liver — infection prophylaxis

  • CMV: D+/R− → valganciclovir 900 mg PO daily (renal-adjusted) × 3–6 months. R+ → valganciclovir × 3 months OR weekly CMV PCR-based preemptive strategy. D−/R− → no CMV ppx; consider acyclovir for HSV.
  • PJP: TMP-SMX SS PO daily (DS 3×/wk acceptable) × 6–12 months.
  • Antifungal: targeted to high-risk recipients only — MELD >30, retransplant, prolonged OR (>10–12 h), massive transfusion, reoperation, choledochojejunostomy, renal failure or RRT, or fulminant indication. Fluconazole 400 mg IV/PO daily × 4 wk OR echinocandin (anidulafungin/micafungin/caspofungin) for invasive candida coverage in critically ill.
  • HBV (recipient): entecavir or tenofovir ± HBIg per hepatology; coordinate dosing with transplant pharmacy.

Watch: Fluconazole inhibits CYP3A4 — expect to reduce tacrolimus dose ~50% on initiation and recheck trough within 48–72 h.

Liver — VTE / GI / stress ulcer prophylaxis

  • SCDs immediately at admission.
  • Pharmacologic VTE ppx [LOCAL PROTOCOL — q12h]: liver recipients are uniquely complex — paradoxically, post-OLT recipients have rebalanced hemostasis with elevated thrombosis risk. Start UFH 5000 units SC q12h on POD 1–2 once surgical bleeding controlled and platelets >50K, INR trending down. Textbook q8h is not used at this center per attending preference.
  • ASA [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: do NOT auto-include on the post-op order set. Uncheck the ASA box; attending decides at the post-op debrief whether/when to start.
  • PPI: pantoprazole 40 mg IV/PO daily while on high-dose steroids.
  • Bowel regimen: docusate 100 mg PO BID + senna 8.6 mg PO QHS PRN.

Liver — organ-specific monitoring

ICU-level monitoring (POD 0–2)

  • Q1h vitals, hourly UOP, arterial line, CVP. Extubate when criteria met.
  • Labs: CBC, CMP, INR, LDH, ammonia, ABG, lactate q6h on POD 0; transition to q12h then daily as stable.
  • Tac trough: start daily once tacrolimus initiated.
  • Doppler US of allograft: POD 1 baseline (HA waveform/RI, PV velocity, HV/IVC patency). Repeat for any unexplained transaminase rise, INR worsening, or hemodynamic concern.

Trend interpretation

  • Expected: AST/ALT peak POD 1–2 (often into thousands), then halve daily; INR normalizes by POD 3–5; bilirubin lags.
  • Red flags: AST/ALT plateau or rise after POD 3, INR not correcting, lactate climbing, drain output sanguineous → urgent doppler ± cross-sectional imaging ± OR.
  • Drain output: quantify daily; check drain bilirubin if biliary leak suspected (drain bili >3× serum bili = leak per consensus).
  • Biopsy threshold: unexplained LFT rise after vascular and biliary causes excluded — protocol or indication biopsy per local pathway.

Evidence: AASLD/AST 2024 Practice Guidance and ILTS-ILCA 2024 consensus both endorse routine early doppler within 24 h of OLT and PRN for graft dysfunction. The recommendation has been stable across the 2012, 2018, and 2024 iterations.

Kidney transplant — adult inpatient

KDIGO 2009 (with the 2020 update on candidate evaluation) remains the most rigorously evidence-graded guideline framework available and is the spine of this section. Living-donor recipients differ from deceased-donor primarily in the risk of delayed graft function (DGF) and the urgency of the diuretic phase.

Kidney — immunosuppression

Induction — risk-stratified

  • KDIGO recommends induction with a biologic agent in all kidney recipients (1A).
  • Standard / lower immunologic risk (no DSA, low PRA, primary transplant, white recipient) → IL-2 receptor antagonist: basiliximab 20 mg IV on POD 0 and POD 4 (KDIGO 1B).
  • Higher immunologic risk (PRA elevated, DSA, retransplant, ABO-incompatible, AA recipient, anticipated DGF, paired exchange) → lymphocyte-depleting agent: rabbit ATG 1.5 mg/kg/dose IV × 3–7 doses (cumulative typical 4.5–6 mg/kg) (KDIGO 2B). [CENTER-SPECIFIC] for cumulative dose.
  • rATG premedication: methylprednisolone, acetaminophen, diphenhydramine before each dose; first dose ideally given intraop or in PACU.

Maintenance

  • Tacrolimus starting 0.05–0.1 mg/kg PO BID; targets typical month 1: 8–10 ng/mL; months 2–3: 6–8 ng/mL; >3 mo: 5–7 ng/mL. [CENTER-SPECIFIC]
  • MMF 1000 mg PO BID.
  • Steroids [LOCAL PROTOCOL — universal taper]: methylpred 500 mg IV intraop → 250 mg IV POD 1 → 125 mg IV POD 2 → then PO prednisone (typically 30 → 20 → 10 → 5 mg by month 3). Early steroid withdrawal (by POD 7) is a published alternative in low-risk patients receiving rATG induction (KDIGO 2C), but the local default is the universal IV taper above.
  • Thymo-held bridge [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: if thymo (rATG) is being held mid-course for thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, or hypotension, continue the IV MP taper through 125 mg POD 2, then transition to 62.5 mg IV daily until the thymo course is complete. If the patient receives only 1 dose of thymo post-op, continue the IV MP taper on schedule rather than abbreviating it.
  • MMF discharge dose [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: inpatient MMF is 1000 mg PO BID. At discharge, dose is individualized — default is often 500 mg BID, but bump to 1 g BID for highly sensitized recipients, DSA-positive recipients, high-BMI recipients, or recipients whose tacrolimus is not yet therapeutic. Ask the attending at discharge if any uncertainty.

Evidence: KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for the Care of Kidney Transplant Recipients. Am J Transplant. 2009;9(Suppl 3):S1–S155. Chapters 1–2 cover induction and initial maintenance; the 2020 candidate evaluation update did not modify these recommendations materially.

Kidney — infection prophylaxis

  • CMV — D+/R−: valganciclovir 900 mg PO daily (renal-dose) × 6 months OR letermovir 480 mg PO daily × 28 weeks. Letermovir was FDA-approved for kidney D+/R− in June 2023 and is endorsed as an alternative first-line option in the 4th International CMV Consensus (2025); it has lower leukopenia/neutropenia than valganciclovir and does not require renal adjustment. Valganciclovir remains the most familiar default. [CENTER-SPECIFIC]
  • CMV — R+ (any donor): valganciclovir 900 mg PO daily × 3–6 months OR preemptive PCR strategy.
  • CMV — D−/R−: no CMV ppx.
  • If T-cell depleting induction used, extend CMV ppx to 6 months regardless of low-risk serostatus combinations (4th Intl CMV Consensus 2025).
  • PJP: TMP-SMX SS PO daily × 6–12 months (KDIGO 1B; Fishman/AST IDCOP 2019). Adds value as UTI ppx (kidney recipients) which is why daily SS is often chosen over DS 3×/wk.
  • Antifungal (oral): nystatin S&S 5 mL QID × 1–3 months while on steroids.
  • UTI surveillance: urinalysis with culture if symptomatic or temperature spike; ureteral stent typically removed at 4–6 weeks per surgical preference.

Kidney — VTE / GI / stress ulcer prophylaxis

  • SCDs at admission.
  • Pharmacologic VTE ppx [LOCAL PROTOCOL — q12h]: UFH 5000 units SC q12h starting POD 1 once surgical hemostasis confirmed. Avoid LMWH if eGFR <30 unless dose-adjusted.
  • ASA [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: do NOT auto-include on the post-op order set. Uncheck the ASA box; attending decides at the post-op debrief.
  • PPI: pantoprazole 40 mg IV/PO daily while on high-dose steroids.
  • Bowel regimen: docusate + senna scheduled.

Kidney — organ-specific monitoring

Standard cadence

  • Q4–6h vitals, strict I/O, hourly UOP for first 24–48 h.
  • Labs: BMP, CBC, Mg, Phos q12h on POD 0–1 then daily. Daily tac trough.
  • Doppler US: POD 1 baseline (RI, arterial/venous patency, peri-graft fluid). Repeat urgently for any acute oliguria or unexplained Cr rise.

Diuretic phase / fluid management

  • Match UOP mL-for-mL with replacement (typically 1/2 NS or per output composition) for first 24–48 h while polyuric.
  • Replace K+/Mg/Phos aggressively — expect significant urinary losses, especially early.

Delayed graft function (DGF)

  • Definition (working): need for dialysis within first 7 days post-transplant, OR failure of Cr to fall by >10% per day for 3 consecutive days.
  • Workup if oliguric/anuric at 24 h or Cr not falling: (1) verify foley not blocked / bladder not distended; (2) doppler US to exclude vascular thrombosis or stenosis and urine leak/collection; (3) consider biopsy if persistent beyond 5–7 days to distinguish ATN vs. acute rejection.
  • Hold/reduce CNI temporarily is a common bridging strategy in severe DGF — [CENTER-SPECIFIC]; some programs maintain low-target tacrolimus and add belatacept rescue.

Biopsy thresholds

  • Indication biopsy: sustained Cr rise >25% from baseline without alternative explanation, persistent DGF >5–7 days, new proteinuria, suspected rejection.
  • Protocol biopsy: timing varies — [CENTER-SPECIFIC].

BK polyomavirus surveillance (Second Intl Consensus 2024)

BK virus reactivation is a kidney-specific complication of immunosuppression and a leading non-rejection cause of graft loss. There is no effective antiviral prophylaxis (fluoroquinolones did not prove effective and are not recommended). The lever is immunosuppression reduction, triggered by quantitative plasma BK PCR surveillance.

  • Screen quantitative plasma BKPyV-DNA: monthly through month 9 post-transplant, then every 3 months until 2 years post-transplant. Surveillance is most important during the inpatient phase only insofar as obtaining a baseline; the heavy lift happens in clinic.
  • Action thresholds: sustained DNAemia >10⁴ copies/mL → reduce IS, typically antimetabolite first (MMF dose-reduction or hold), then CNI dose-reduction if DNAemia persists. Lower thresholds (>10³) prompt closer surveillance.
  • Biopsy: indicated for sustained high-level DNAemia or unexplained Cr rise — distinguishes BK nephropathy from rejection, which can coexist.

Reference: Kotton CN et al. The Second International Consensus Guidelines on the Management of BK Polyomavirus in Kidney Transplantation. Transplantation. 2024;108(9):1834–1866.

Pancreas transplant — adult inpatient

Pancreas transplant (SPK, PAK, or PTA) carries a meaningfully higher early-graft-loss rate than kidney or liver, dominated by graft thrombosis (the leading cause of early loss). The protocol below assumes enteric drainage with whole-organ pancreas plus a kidney (SPK) is the modal case; isolated pancreas (PAK/PTA) is annotated where it diverges.

Pancreas — immunosuppression

Induction

  • Lymphocyte-depleting induction is standard (used in the large majority of US pancreas transplants per OPTN/SRTR data).
  • Rabbit ATG 1–1.5 mg/kg/dose × 4–7 doses (cumulative 4.5–7.5 mg/kg) is the most common regimen. [CENTER-SPECIFIC]
  • Alemtuzumab 30 mg IV × 1 is an alternative used at some programs.

Maintenance

  • Tacrolimus trough targets typical month 1: 10–12 ng/mL; months 2–3: 8–10; >3 mo: 6–8. Higher than kidney/liver because of higher immunologic risk and tendency for early rejection. [CENTER-SPECIFIC]
  • MMF 1000 mg PO BID.
  • Steroids [LOCAL PROTOCOL — universal IV taper]: methylpred 500 mg IV intraop → 250 mg IV POD 1 → 125 mg IV POD 2 → then PO prednisone taper. Some programs run steroid-free long-term maintenance with rATG induction; others maintain low-dose prednisone 5 mg. Long-term maintenance approach [CENTER-SPECIFIC].
  • Thymo-held bridge [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: if thymo is held mid-course (cytopenias, hypotension), continue the IV MP taper through 125 mg POD 2, then transition to 62.5 mg IV daily until the thymo course is complete. If only 1 thymo dose given, continue the IV MP taper as scheduled.
  • MMF discharge dose [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: inpatient MMF is 1000 mg PO BID. At discharge, individualize — default may be 500 mg BID, but bump to 1 g BID for highly sensitized / DSA-positive / high-BMI / sub-therapeutic-tacrolimus recipients. Ask the attending at discharge.

Evidence: OPTN/SRTR 2023 Annual Data Report: Pancreas. Am J Transplant 2025 (915 pancreas transplants in 2023; lymphocyte-depleting induction remains predominant; thrombosis remains the leading cause of early graft loss). Niederhaus SV. Pancreas transplant outcomes. Curr Opin Organ Transplant 2015;20:94–102 — older but still useful narrative review.

Pancreas — infection prophylaxis

  • CMV: D+/R− → valganciclovir 900 mg PO daily × 6 months. R+ → valganciclovir × 3–6 months. Because nearly all pancreas recipients receive lymphocyte-depleting induction, default to 6 months even for moderate-risk serostatus combinations (4th Intl CMV Consensus 2025). Letermovir is currently FDA-approved only for kidney D+/R− and is NOT yet endorsed for pancreas; valganciclovir remains the default.
  • PJP: TMP-SMX SS PO daily × 12 months (some programs continue indefinitely given depletion + ongoing IS intensity).
  • Antifungal: enteric-drained pancreas — fluconazole 400 mg IV/PO daily × 7–14 days (intra-abdominal candida risk from duodenal segment); echinocandin if critically ill or recent azole exposure.
  • Anti-anaerobic / broad surgical coverage: piperacillin-tazobactam (or equivalent) intraop and continued 5–7 days post-op given enteric anastomosis. [CENTER-SPECIFIC]

Watch: Fluconazole + tacrolimus interaction is large — reduce tac dose ~50% on day of fluconazole start and check trough within 48 h.

Pancreas — anticoagulation (organ-specific)

Graft thrombosis is the leading cause of early pancreas allograft loss. Anticoagulation is the highest-stakes decision in pancreas postop care.

  • Heparin infusion (low-intensity, NO bolus) starting POD 0–1 once surgical hemostasis confirmed. Goal aPTT typically 40–60 sec (sub-therapeutic intensity). Continue 5–7 days. [CENTER-SPECIFIC] — some programs use therapeutic heparin if hypercoagulable, others use prophylactic-dose UFH SC + ASA only.
  • ASA transition [LOCAL PROTOCOL]: do NOT auto-transition to ASA 81 mg at end of gtt. ASA initiation is an attending decision at the post-op debrief or on transition off the gtt — uncheck the ASA box on the order set; attending will sign off when appropriate.
  • Hct/Hgb monitoring q6–12h for first 48–72 h — bleeding is the competing complication.
  • Doppler US POD 1 baseline + at any concern for thrombosis (sudden glucose rise, abdominal pain, drain output change).

Pancreas — VTE / GI / stress ulcer prophylaxis

  • SCDs at admission. Pharmacologic VTE ppx is generally subsumed under the heparin infusion regimen above.
  • PPI: pantoprazole 40 mg IV/PO daily while on steroids.
  • Bowel regimen: docusate + senna scheduled; close attention because pancreas recipients have an enteric anastomosis.

Pancreas — organ-specific monitoring

Glucose control

  • Q1h fingerstick glucose for first 24 h post-reperfusion; transition to Q2h then Q4h as glucose stabilizes.
  • Target glucose 80–150 mg/dL in the immediate post-op period; insulin infusion as needed for elevations.
  • Glycemic excursion is the earliest sign of graft thrombosis — unexpected sustained hyperglycemia mandates urgent doppler.

Enzyme monitoring

  • Amylase and lipase q6–12h × 48 h then daily. A graft enzyme bump POD 1–2 is expected (reperfusion); persistent elevation or rebound merits imaging.
  • Drain amylase checked when output character changes — markedly elevated drain amylase suggests anastomotic leak.

Doppler US

  • POD 1 baseline doppler evaluating arterial inflow, splenic/SMA waveforms, venous outflow (portal/iliac), parenchymal perfusion.
  • Repeat doppler urgently for: sudden hyperglycemia, drop in Hct without surgical bleeding, drain output change, abdominal pain disproportionate to exam.

Renal monitoring (if SPK)

  • Apply the kidney protocol section in parallel — Cr trending, UOP, doppler of renal allograft.

Citations (v0.3 — literature-checked May 2026 + local conventions integrated)

Kidney transplantation

  • KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for the Care of Kidney Transplant Recipients. Am J Transplant. 2009;9(Suppl 3):S1–S155. (Still current — no successor published.)
  • Chadban SJ et al. KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline on the Evaluation and Management of Candidates for Kidney Transplantation. Transplantation. 2020;104(4S1):S11–S103. (Pre-transplant evaluation; complementary scope.)

Liver transplantation

  • AASLD/AST Practice Guideline. Adult Liver Transplantation: Diagnosis and Management of Graft-Related Complications. Te HS, Agopian VG, Demetris AJ, et al. Liver Transpl. 2025;32(3):444–490. doi:10.1097/LVT.0000000000000715. (Now formally published; supersedes the interim Hepatology 2024 online version and Lucey 2013.)
  • The 2024 ILTS-ILCA Consensus Recommendations for Liver Transplantation and Transplant Oncology. Liver Transplantation. 2025;31(6). (Supersedes Charlton 2018.)
  • EASL Clinical Practice Guidelines on Liver Transplantation. J Hepatol. 2024 (October). (European reference; covers machine perfusion, frailty, allocation equity.)

Infection prophylaxis

  • Kotton CN et al. The Fourth International Consensus Guidelines on the Management of Cytomegalovirus in Solid Organ Transplantation. Transplantation. 2025;109(7). (Supersedes Razonable/Humar AST IDCOP 2019; endorses letermovir as alternative first-line for D+/R− kidney.)
  • Kotton CN et al. The Second International Consensus Guidelines on the Management of BK Polyomavirus in Kidney Transplantation. Transplantation. 2024;108(9):1834–1866. (New standard for BK screening; no effective drug prophylaxis.)
  • Fishman JA et al. Pneumocystis jirovecii in solid organ transplantation — Guidelines of the AST IDCOP. Clin Transplant. 2019;33:e13587. (Still current; no superseding document identified May 2026.)
  • Aslam S et al. Candida infections in solid organ transplantation — Guidelines of the AST IDCOP. Clin Transplant. 2019;33:e13623. (Still current; no superseding document identified May 2026.)

Pancreas transplantation

  • OPTN/SRTR 2023 Annual Data Report: Pancreas. Am J Transplant. 2025. https://srtr.transplant.hrsa.gov (915 pancreas transplants in 2023; thrombosis remains leading cause of early graft loss.)
  • Niederhaus SV. Pancreas transplant outcomes. Curr Opin Organ Transplant. 2015;20:94–102. (Older narrative review; useful for historical framing.)

Therapeutics — drug-specific

  • Letermovir (Prevymis) — FDA approved June 2023 for prevention of CMV disease in adult kidney transplant recipients at high risk (D+/R−). 480 mg PO/IV daily through day 200 (~28 weeks). No renal adjustment required.

Notes on currency

Three of the v0.1 citations were superseded by 2024–2025 publications and have been replaced. Two (Fishman PJP 2019 and Aslam Candida 2019) remain current as of May 2026 per literature review. The KDIGO 2009 kidney care document, despite its age, has not been replaced; KDIGO has confirmed in subsequent communications that it remains the active reference. Any future v0.x of this protocol should re-check these citations annually — the AST IDCOP series is overdue for a 4th-edition refresh and may publish updates within the next 12 months.
